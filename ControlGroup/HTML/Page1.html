Dear participant,

thank you for considering participating in a research study on the topic of "Time based interventions in ITS" of the Department of Computational Methods in Modeling and Analysis of Learning Processes at the University of Duisburg-Essen. This research is carried out as part of the Master’s thesis of Nadiya Davydova with the title “Implementation and Evaluation of Response Time and Performance Based Interventions in Intelligent Tutoring Systems”.

The aim of this study is to understand how intelligent tutoring systems could be optimized to improve the overall learning experience of students. We are particularly interested in the relation between the time students spend working on a task and their performance. Additionally, we want to investigate the effect of time-relevant pedagogical interventions, such as timely feedback. As a part of the study, you will learn about the topic “Radix Sort” and you will be asked to solve a few tasks. In the end, you will be asked to fill out a questionnaire about your experience with the tutoring system. The overall duration of the study is approximately 45-60 minutes. No previous knowledge is required for participation.

You can withdraw from the study at any point, without having to give a reason. If any questions during the study make you feel uncomfortable, you do not have to answer them. Withdrawing from the study will have no effect on you. If you withdraw from the study, we will not retain the information you have given thus far, unless you are happy for us to do so.

We will use the collected information for the purposes of the Master’s thesis of Nadiya Davydova with the title “Implementation and Evaluation of Response Time and Performance Based Interventions in Intelligent Tutoring Systems” , academic publications and for informing future research. The records from this study will be kept confidential. Only the people involved in this study will have access to the material generated from this study. All statements and answers, as well as all the data that is collected, will be analyzed in anonymous form. Your name will not be used in any reports or publications resulting from the study. Any hard copies of research information will be kept in locked files at all times. Original data will be destroyed after it has served its initial purpose.

If you have any questions regarding this study, please contact Nadiya Davydova on nadiya.davydova@stud.uni.due.de.
